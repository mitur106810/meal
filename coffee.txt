Ethiopia is the birthplace of Arabica coffee, where it has grown wild for millennia. Today, it is Africa's largest producer and is renowned for its diverse coffee regions, such as Yirgacheffe and Sidamo, which offer unique floral and citrus profiles.

Notable Coffee Brands & Roasters
Several companies provide high-quality Ethiopian coffee, focusing on sustainable practices and traditional roasting methods. 
Garden of Coffee: An artisan hand-roaster in Addis Ababa that uses traditional ceramic roasters. According to gardenofcoffee.com, they roast beans only upon order to ensure maximum freshness.
Moyee Coffee Ethiopia: Known for its "FairChain" model, Moyee pays a 20% premium to farmers and roasts beans locally in Africa to keep more value within the community.
Aster Bunna: This company specializes in world-renowned Ethiopian beans including Yergacheffe, Kaffa, and Harar. According to asterbunna.com, they supply hotels, embassies, and supermarkets globally.
Tomoca Coffee (TO.MO.CA): Established in 1953, it is the oldest and most iconic roaster in Ethiopia. It is considered a "gold standard" for coffee in Addis Ababa.
